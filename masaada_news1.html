EXTOLLO INTERNATIONAL - HAITI

Today in Haiti, 74% of the workforce are underemployed while 8.5 million people live on less than $2.41 per day. Since the 2010 earthquake, over $13 billion dollars have poured into Haiti with little sustainable impact. Extollo is changing that and Msaada is assisting.

Extollo International (Extollo) www.extollo.org is a faith-based social enterprise that is unleashing Haiti’s potential to build a stronger future by training a construction workforce of character and capacity while adding strength and safety to Haiti’s infrastructure.
Learn more about Extollo

Concrete Tilt-Up Panelized Housing

How do you create an affordable, safe living environment that is locally sourced and built; earthquake and wind-resistant; termite and fireproof as well as energy-efficient and sustainable? After witnessing the destruction of the earthquake in Haiti, Sherman Balch was led by God to use his talents and resources to derive the solution. An internationally-recognized expert in Tilt-up construction technology, Sherman has created the means to manufacture these homes using local workers trained by Extollo International in Bercy, Haiti. With professional assistance from Msaada, Extollo International will be meeting a huge humanitarian need in one of the poorest countries in the world.

Sherman Balch, Founder of Extollo, commented on the project and working with Msaada: “Msaada has provided a full range of services for Extollo’s Construction Training Center, from conceptual layout, topography for grading, construction plans, elevations and renderings of the finished products. We look forward to continuing working with Msaada as we develop our prototype Tilt-up housing project…the goal being to develop a low-cost earthquake and hurricane resistant home, built by Haitians for Haitians."
